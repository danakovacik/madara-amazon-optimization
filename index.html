Introduction

The opportunity on Amazon US

The US is one of the most competitive — and most rewarding — beauty markets on Amazon. Shoppers discover, compare and convert differently here than they do in the EU, and the current MÁDARA presence has meaningful headroom across content, catalog structure, storefront and promotional activity.

This document distills our audit into a clear, categorized roadmap. Each section identifies the gap, explains why it matters for US shoppers, and recommends the work to close it. Together, these moves are designed to improve discoverability, conversion, average order value and customer retention in a measurable way.

CATEGORY · Product Imagery
PILLAR · CONTENT & CREATIVE

Make images do the selling — and the SEO

There is significant room to elevate product graphics and the text layered onto them. Done well, images convert browsers into buyers and help Amazon's algorithm understand the listing.

Using RUFUS (Amazon AI) where customers ask question about the product is an impactful tool to get ideas as well on the valuable content that is worth asking

What we recommend adding to every listing

  • Concerns the product targets (e.g., how the concealer addresses dark circles, redness, or blemishes).
  • A clear guide to finding the right undertone — cool, warm, neutral — visualized on real skin.
  • Application tips and usage technique, step by step, so the shopper understands how to get the intended result.
  • Real faces in different shades, named where possible — e.g., "Beth wears #35 Honey." Personalization makes the shade range feel lived-in and trustworthy.
  • Product benefits:
    • when to apply (AM/PM) and how long the product lasts on the skin.
    • Organic product
    • Waterproof not waterproof
    • UV protection or not

WHY THIS MATTERS

Amazon's image real estate is some of the most valuable in e-commerce. Each slot is an opportunity to answer a question, overcome an objection, or teach the shopper why MÁDARA is the right choice. Strong imagery also feeds Amazon SEO through the alt and embedded text.

CATEGORY · Catalog Architecture
PILLAR · CATALOG

Variant merging and Brand Story — the quiet conversion lever

How products are structured on Amazon directly influences whether a shopper sees the full range. Two fixes move the needle quickly.

Proper variant merging

All Retinol SKUs should sit under the same parent ASIN — even across day/night and size variants. When a shopper lands on one, they should see the full family, with the variant theme set correctly. This single change typically lifts conversion because it keeps the customer inside MÁDARA rather than bouncing to a competitor.

Complete Brand Story on every US PDP

Brand Story must appear on every product available in the US. It reinforces trust and ties the catalog together. The products below are currently missing it:

CATEGORY · Bundle Strategy
PILLAR · CATALOG

Create bundles that fit how shoppers already think

The concealer image already references a duo with foundation — that intent is unmet if the bundle itself does not exist. Bundles raise AOV, improve attach-rates, and give shoppers a clear next step.

Where to start

  • Build the duos and trios already referenced in existing imagery (e.g., concealer + foundation).
  • Offer a modest bundle discount to justify the multi-buy.
  • Cluster bundles by routine: full-face makeup, day-to-night skincare, body ritual.
  • By utilizing the Amazon Market Basket Analysis tool, we can identify high-conversion bundling opportunities. This data reveals the specific 'combination percentage' of your products, pinpointing exactly which items customers naturally prefer to purchase together.
WHY BUNDLES WORK

Bundles lift both the individual SKU and the overall brand — customers discover products they would not have searched for on their own, and AOV rises without paid acquisition cost. It is important to showcase bundles in the Brand story, in the store page and add them as well in the a+content to increase visibility.

CATEGORY · Brand Store
PILLAR · STOREFRONT

A storefront designed for discovery, not just display

The current Brand Store is essentially a single “shop all” page. That underplays the breadth of the MÁDARA range and offers no guided paths for customers who come to discover rather than to search.

A recommended store structure

  • Home page: A high-performing homepage should do two things: show the customer the full range of what they can buy and tell them why they should buy from you. Elevating the organic benefits and showcasing your wider range helps build immediate brand trust and encourages customers to explore beyond just a single product, ultimately increasing their lifetime value.
  • Best sellers / trending page.
  • Bundles, gifts and sets page.
  • Deals / discounts page (when applicable).
  • New releases.

Dedicated category pages

  • Eyes.
  • Face.
  • Lips.
  • Skincare.

CATEGORY · Lifecycle Promotions
PILLAR · PROMOTIONS

Brand-Tailored Promotions — four audiences, four goals

Amazon's Brand-Tailored Promotions allow us to move away from 'blunt' discounts that cut into your margins for every customer. Instead, we can now target shoppers based on their specific relationship with Madara, turning promotions into a precision tool for growth and loyalty.

Why This Matters for the Brand (The Impact)

  • Higher Conversion with Lower Margin Loss: By offering discounts only to high-intent groups (like Basket Abandoners or In-Market Customers), we aren't wasting money on shoppers who would have paid full price anyway. We only use the discount when it is strictly necessary to "close the deal".
  • Algorithm Boost: Amazon's search algorithm rewards high conversion rates. When we send targeted 10-15% offers to people already interested in Madara, our conversion rate spikes. This sends a positive signal to Amazon, which can improve our organic ranking across the entire catalog.
  • Increased Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): We can reward Repeat Customers with exclusive "Loyalty" discounts. This prevents them from switching to a competitor and keeps them within the Madara ecosystem, making each customer significantly more profitable over time.
  • Zero Upfront Cost: Unlike Lightning Deals or Prime Member Discounts, there are no participation fees for Brand-Tailored Promotions. We only pay when a customer actually makes a purchase, making this one of the most cost-effective marketing tools on the platform.

The four plays we recommend

  • New customer acquisition · Acquire and convert shoppers interested in the brand but who have not yet purchased.
  • Customer retention · Improve loyalty and repeat purchase from existing buyers.
  • Re-engagement · Win back lost and churned customers.
  • Cross-sell · Introduce complementary products to existing customers — often the most profitable of the four.

CATEGORY · Peak Events & Traffic
PILLAR · PROMOTIONS

Own the moments that move the market

The US market is event-driven. A very large share of annual sales concentrates around a few tent-pole moments, and Amazon's promotional engine rewards participation with traffic.

Non-negotiable events

  • July Prime Day.
  • October Prime Day.
  • Black Friday / Cyber Monday (BFCM).

Supporting playbook

Participation alone is not enough. Each event should be supported with external traffic: social, email, influencer and any brand-led channels. This signals demand to Amazon's algorithm and compounds organic visibility long after the event ends.

THE COMPOUNDING EFFECT

Events that are supported with external traffic tend to rank better in the weeks and months that follow. Treat each tent-pole as a multi-week campaign, not a single-day promotion.
